### GAP #1: HOME CLEANING SERVICES

#### 1.1 Topic Overview

Home cleaning on Airtasker encompasses a range of residential cleaning services including regular maintenance cleaning, deep/spring cleaning, end-of-lease/bond cleaning (critical in Sydney rental market), move-in/move-out cleaning, and specialized services like oven/fridge cleaning.

**Typical Tasks Include:**
- General room cleaning (dust, vacuum, mop)
- Kitchen cleaning (surface sanitization, appliance exterior cleaning)
- Bathroom deep cleaning (showers, toilets, mirrors)
- Window cleaning (interior/exterior where accessible)
- Carpet steam cleaning (often sub-contracted or requires equipment)
- Bond cleaning: Comprehensive cleaning to meet rental agreement standards including detailed attention to fixtures, grout, and hidden areas

**Industry Standards & Best Practices:**
- **End-of-lease cleaning** in Sydney must meet REINSW/RTA standards - failures result in bond deductions ($200-$500+ common)
- Professional cleaners typically work in pairs for efficiency and safety
- Bond back guarantee is standard (return to fix any issues)
- Eco-friendly products increasingly requested (especially in inner Sydney)
- COVID-19 protocols remain expected in many households
- Professional invoice/receipts required for bond claims

#### 1.2 Getting Started (Day 1 - Month 1)

**Day 1 Essentials:**

What a total beginner learns first:
1. **Product Knowledge**: Understanding which chemicals work on which surfaces (pH levels, material compatibility)
2. **Equipment Basics**: High-quality microfiber cloths (colour-coded: red bathroom, blue general, yellow kitchen, green glass), squeegees, scrubbing brushes, extendable dusters
3. **Room Sequence**: Top-to-bottom, back-to-front cleaning pattern to avoid re-contamination
4. **Time Estimation**: Basic timing - 1 bedroom unit = 2-3 hours, 3 bedroom house = 4-6 hours

**Core Fundamentals Required:**
- Surface material identification (marble vs granite vs laminate - different care requirements)
- Chemical safety and proper dilution ratios
- Efficiency techniques (systematic patterns, tool organization)
- Client communication (setting expectations, managing scope creep)
- Basic business skills (invoicing, tax obligations for ABN holders)

**Common Pitfalls to Avoid:**
- Using abrasive cleaners on delicate surfaces (scratches = liability)
- Mixing bleach with ammonia (creates toxic gas - serious hazard)
- Underestimating time (common: quoting 2 hours for 4 hours of work)
- Skipping corners/edges (inspectors always check)
- Not clarifying scope ("clean the house" varies wildly by client expectation)
- Working without insurance (accidents happen; broken items = costs)
- Using client's supplies without permission (some want specific products)

### GAP #2: GARDENING & LANDSCAPING

#### 2.1 Topic Overview

Gardening and landscaping on Airtasker ranges from basic maintenance (mowing, weeding) to complex projects (landscape design, retaining walls). In Sydney's climate, outdoor services are year-round with seasonal variations.

**Typical Tasks Include:**
- **Maintenance**: Lawn mowing, edging, hedge trimming, weeding, mulching, pruning
- **Clean-up**: Green waste removal, garden tidy-ups, end-of-lease garden preparation
- **Planting**: Seasonal planting, garden beds, potted arrangements
- **Landscaping**: Basic leveling, path laying, garden design, retaining walls (unlicensed minor works)
- **Specialized**: Tree trimming (small-medium only - large trees require arborist license), irrigation repairs, top dressing
- **Seasonal**: Leaf removal (autumn), storm cleanup, pre-sale garden prep

**Industry Standards & Best Practices:**
- **Height regulations**: Lawn mowing heights vary by grass type (Sir Walter Buffalo: 25-50mm, Couch: 10-25mm)
- **Pruning seasons**: Native plants (after flowering), deciduous (winter), citrus (spring)
- **Green waste**: Legal disposal required - council bins, tip runs, or commercial green waste services
- **Tool maintenance**: Clean tools between jobs (disease prevention)
- **Soil knowledge**: pH testing, fertilizer application rates, drainage assessment
- **Mulch standards**: 50-75mm depth for weed suppression, keeping away from plant stems
- **Sydney climate awareness**: Drought-tolerant design knowledge essential (water restrictions history)

#### 2.2 Getting Started (Day 1 - Month 1)

**Day 1 Essentials:**

What a total beginner learns first:
1. **Plant Identification**: Basic recognition of weeds vs. wanted plants (critical - pulling the wrong thing = liability)
2. **Tool Safety**: Proper handling of mowers, edgers, hedge trimmers (cutting yourself = hospital visit)
3. **Body Mechanics**: Lifting technique (bags of mulch = 20-30kg each; back injuries common)
4. **Weather Reading**: Understanding when not to work (extreme heat in Sydney = dangerous)

**Core Fundamentals Required:**
- Native Australian plant identification (Sydney has unique flora)
- Weed identification (Oxalis, bindii, clover, dandelion, couch grass, kikuyu runners)
- Basic lawnmower maintenance (blade sharpening, oil changes)
- Stihl/Makita battery tool operation (industry standard)
- Mulch types and applications
- Poisonous plant awareness (oleander, coral tree, stinging nettle)
- Snake/spider awareness (Sydney's dangerous wildlife - redbacks, funnel-webs, brown snakes)
- Chemical handling ( glyphosate/weedkiller safety)

**Common Pitfalls to Avoid:**
- **Poisoning plants**: Over-fertilizing, wrong chemical on wrong plant
- **Liability**: Damaging client's property (broken windows from mowers, damaged retic)
- **Underestimating time**: "Quick tidy" often reveals hours of work
- **Disposal issues**: Leaving green waste without arrangement
- **Over-committing**: Taking on jobs requiring equipment you don't have
- **Inappropriate tools**: Using hedge trimmers on branches too thick (equipment damage)
- **Weather blindness**: Working in 40° heat (heatstroke risk)
- **Not checking for wildlife**: Disturbing possums, bird nests (protected species)
- **Council regulations**: Removing trees without checking exempt species (fines possible)

### GAP #3: PHOTOGRAPHY SERVICES

#### 3.1 Topic Overview

Photography on Airtasker encompasses event photography (weddings, parties), portrait sessions (headshots, family), product photography (e-commerce), real estate photography, content creation (influencers/businesses), and specialized niches (pet photography, newborn).

**Typical Tasks Include:**
- **Event Photography**: Weddings, birthdays, corporate events, engagement parties
- **Portrait Sessions**: Individual headshots, family portraits, couple sessions
- **Real Estate**: Property listings, Airbnb photography, architectural detail
- **Product Photography**: E-commerce listings, small business products, flat lays
- **Content Creation**: Social media content for businesses/influencers, lifestyle shoots
- **Newborn/Family**: In-home lifestyle sessions
- **Pet Photography**: Animal portraits (trending in Sydney)

**Industry Standards & Best Practices:**
- **RAW shooting**: Always shoot RAW for maximum editing flexibility
- **Backup protocol**: Dual card slots or immediate backup - data loss = reputation death
- **Contract/Agreement**: Clear deliverables, timeline, usage rights (copyright crucial)
- **Culling standards**: 10-20% keeper rate typical; deliver best only
- **Editing consistency**: Develop signature style/preset for branding
- **Turnaround time**: 2-4 weeks standard for events, 1 week for portraits
- **Gear redundancy**: Backup camera/batteries/cards for critical events (weddings)
- **Client communication**: Pre-shoot consultation essential for alignment
- **Model releases**: Required for commercial/portfolio use

#### 3.2 Getting Started (Day 1 - Month 1)

**Day 1 Essentials:**

What a total beginner learns first:
1. **Exposure Triangle**: Master manual mode (aperture, shutter speed, ISO relationship)
2. **Focus Techniques**: Single point AF, back-button focus, focus recomposing
3. **Lighting Basics**: Natural light direction (golden hour, open shade, harsh midday avoidance)
4. **Composition Rules**: Rule of thirds, leading lines, negative space

**Core Fundamentals Required:**
- Lightroom/Photoshop editing workflow
- Color theory and white balance mastery
- Lens selection (35mm vs 50mm vs 85mm for portraits)
- Basic posing directives (body positioning, non-verbal communication)
- File management and organization
- Client direction skills (shy subjects need guidance)
- Marketing/self-promotion basics
- Pricing psychology and packaging
- Copyright basics (Australian Copyright Act)

**Common Pitfalls to Avoid:**
- **Underpricing**: "$50 for a wedding" destroys market and doesn't cover costs
- **Over-delivering**: "I'll give you ALL 2000 photos" - client overwhelmed, edit time absurd
- **Gear obsession**: Better camera ≠ better photos (skill matters most)
- **No contract**: Verbal agreements lead to scope creep and disputes
- **Ignoring backups**: Single card slot = playing Russian roulette
- **Over-editing**: Heavy HDR, oversaturation screams amateur
- **Inconsistent style**: Portfolio looks like 5 different photographers
- **Missed moments**: Not capturing the first kiss at wedding = refund territory
- **Poor communication**: Ghosting clients post-shoot destroys reputation
- **Unclear deliverables**: "Some edited photos" vs "50 edited photos in 4K resolution"

**Red Flags That Expose Inexperience:**
